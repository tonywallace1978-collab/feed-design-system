Job Description
Seeking an experienced Robot Programmer to join our team at our Detroit manufacturing facility. The successful candidate will be responsible for programming, troubleshooting, and optimizing FANUC R-2000iC robotic systems in a high-volume automotive production environment. Daily responsibilities include developing new robot programs for welding and material handling applications, modifying existing programs for engineering changes, and collaborating with the controls engineering team on cell integration projects. This is a fast-paced role requiring hands-on experience with FANUC's Karel programming language, proficiency in offline programming tools such as Roboguide, and a strong understanding of safety protocols for industrial robotics. Candidates should demonstrate expertise in multi-robot coordination, vision-guided systems, and path optimization techniques. Experience with arc welding (GMAW/GTAW) robot programming is highly preferred.

Procedures & Policies
All contractors must complete a 4-hour safety orientation on the first day, including lockout/tagout (LOTO) procedures, confined space entry awareness, and emergency evacuation protocols. Personal protective equipment (PPE) including safety glasses, steel-toe boots, and high-visibility vests must be worn at all times on the production floor. All robot teaching and programming must be performed with the robot cell in manual mode at reduced speed (≤250mm/s) unless explicit written authorization is obtained from the Safety Manager for automatic-mode operation. Contractors are required to maintain daily field service reports documenting all work performed, robot program modifications, and any safety incidents or near-misses.
